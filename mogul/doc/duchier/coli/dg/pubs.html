Publications

Any one interested in using TDG must read Ralph Debusmann's Master Thesis A Declarative Grammar Formalism For Dependency Grammar which explains the approach in great detail and presents its application to the modelisation of German syntax. The thesis also contains a detailed description of the concrete grammar specification language.

A Declarative Grammar Formalism For Dependency Grammar
Ralph Debusmann. Master Thesis, October 2001, Universität des Saarlandes, Saarbrücken, Germany.

Lexicalized Syntax and Topology for Non-projective Dependency Grammar
Denys Duchier. Formal Grammars & Mathematics of Language, FGMOL2001, Helsinki, Finland, 10-12 August 2001.

Topological Dependency Trees: A Constraint-based Account of Linear Precedence
Denys Duchier, Ralph Debusmann. 39th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL 2001), Toulouse, France, July 9-11 2001.

Configuration Of Labeled Trees Under Lexicalized Constraints And Principles
Denys Duchier. To appear in the Journal of Language and Computation, (submitted December 2000).

Axiomatizing Dependency Parsing Using Set Constraints
Denys Duchier. Sixth Meeting on Mathematics of Language (MOL6), pages 115-126, Orlando, Florida, July 1999.
